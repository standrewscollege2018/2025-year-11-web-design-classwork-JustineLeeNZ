The Beatles

The Beatles were an English rock band formed in Liverpool in 1960. The band members were John Lennon, Paul McCartney, George Harrison and Ringo Starr. They became known as The Fab Four. They were hugely successful and very influential band as well as being the best-selling band in history. They finally called it quits in 1970.

The band members

John Lennon

John Lennon was born in 1940. He formed the band The Quarrymen in 1957
He played guitar, sang and composed.
He was murdered in 1980 by a disgruntled fan

Paul McCartney

Sir Paul McCartney was born in 1942. He started out playing trumpet but quickly converted to guitar. He also played keyboard and drums. He joined The Quarrymen in 1957.
He continued a successful career in music after the breakup of The Beatles.
He was knighted for services to music in 1997.

George Harrison

George Harrison was born in 1943. He joined Lennon and McCartney in The Quarrymen in 1958.
He is best known as lead guitarist for The Beatles but also composed several of their songs.
He co-founded the Travelling Willburys in 1988. He died in 2001 of cancer.

Ringo Starr

Sir Richard Starkey (better known as Ringo Starr) was the drummer for The Beatles, joining in 1962. He also sang the lead on several songs
He was knighted for services to music in 2018

Their music

The Beatles released 13 albums and over 200 songs. Some of the most well-know include

  • Yellow Submarine
  • Eleanor Rigby
  • Eight Days A Week
  • Hey Jude
  • Let It Be
  • Penny Lane
